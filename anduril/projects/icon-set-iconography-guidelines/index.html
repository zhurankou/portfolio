01 // Contribution

I took the initiative to create an icon set for eero Insight and led the effort of establishing iconography guidelines.

When I joined the eero team, the main problem they had was the lack of ownership and iconography guidelines. Icons used in production had many imperfections — misalignments, inconsistent patterns across the set, and frequent misuse in the product. Icons were also duplicated, causing customer confusion.

03 // System

I worked with the team on design principles we should follow to create our new icon set. I also established design grids for Navigational (24px) and Table (16px) icons, 2px safe areas, keylines for Circle, Square, Horizontal and Vertical shapes, and basic style requirements like corner radius, angle, and stroke width.

Design Principles

Clarity Keep it clear and useful. Using a reductive approach, find the simplest expression of an object or idea to represent a single, concise message.
Simplicity Use basic, elementary shapes (circle, square, triangle) to elicit our hardware's rounded forms, as a foundation of our design language.
Legibility Icons should be seen comfortably in dense content environments at defined compact sizes.
Consistency Use grid and construction lines, uniformed terminals, joints, stroke thickness (2px), and the level of details across the whole set.
